      <div class="meta-row">
        <span class="meta-label">Category:</span> Coins &amp; Precious Metal
      </div>
      <div class="meta-row">
        <span class="meta-label">Vault ID:</span> COIN-0001
      </div>
      <div class="meta-row">
        <span class="meta-label">Status:</span>
        <span class="status not-for-sale">Not currently listed for sale</span>
      </div>

      <p>
        These U.S. wartime nickels were minted from 1942–1945 during World War II, when nickel metal
        was needed for military equipment. To conserve nickel, the Mint changed the alloy of these
        coins to include real silver, making them both historically significant and interesting to
        collectors.
      </p>

      <ul>
        <li><strong>Mint years:</strong> 1942–1945</li>
        <li><strong>Composition:</strong> 35% silver, 56% copper, 9% manganese</li>
        <li><strong>Design:</strong> Thomas Jefferson on the front; Monticello on the back</li>
        <li><strong>Special feature:</strong> Large mint mark (P, D, or S) above Monticello indicating the wartime silver issue</li>
        <li><strong>Collectibility:</strong> Popular with silver stackers and WWII history collectors; increasingly scarce in circulation</li>
      </ul>
